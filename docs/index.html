Few-shot Domain Transfer for Generative Adversarial Networks
We present Few-shot GAN, a novel approach to adapt a pretrained GAN model to generate new images with only a few training images from a target domain. Inspired by component analysis techniques that have shown to relax training data requirements, we repurpose SVD decomposition on the pretrained GAN's weights to gradually evolve the pretrained GAN into a target-specific GAN. Instead of finetuning the entire GAN or just batch statistics as in alternative methods, our method learns to adjust the obtained singular values to reshape the weight subspace, and strikes a balance between parameter efficiency and diversity. To detect multi-mode overfitting in the few-shot training, we develop a novel metric to monitor latent space smoothness during the training process. Unlike most GANs that often require large-scale training datasets (\ie tens of thousands of images), the proposed method achieves high-quality, diverse image synthesis in a data-efficient setting (\eg as few as 25 images). We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach on both in-domain and cross-domain transfer settings, including face image personalization and image stylization (\eg church$\rightarrow$Van Gogh painting and faces$\rightarrow$portrait painting/anime). We demonstrate qualitative and quantitative results against competing methods across several datasets.
